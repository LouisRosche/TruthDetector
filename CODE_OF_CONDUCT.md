# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Truth Hunters is an educational project designed for middle school students. We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

As contributors and maintainers of this project, we pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- **Be respectful** of differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Use welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Focus on what is best for the community and students**
- **Show empathy toward other community members**
- **Respect educational and pedagogical expertise**
- **Accept constructive criticism gracefully**
- **Prioritize student safety and age-appropriate content**

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

- Harassment, trolling, or discriminatory comments
- Personal attacks or insults
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in an educational or professional setting

**Special Consideration**: Because this project is used with middle school students (ages 11-14), we have zero tolerance for:
- Age-inappropriate content suggestions
- Attempts to bypass content moderation systems
- Proposals that would compromise student safety or privacy

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Contributors who exhibit inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful behavior

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Public spaces when an individual is representing the project or community
- Private communications when addressing project-related matters

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:
- Opening a GitHub issue with the label "Code of Conduct"
- Contacting the project maintainers directly
- Using GitHub's [Report Abuse](https://github.com/contact/report-abuse) feature for serious violations

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Consequences

Contributors who violate this Code of Conduct may face:

1. **Warning**: A private, written warning with clarity around the violation and expected behavior
2. **Temporary Ban**: Temporary restriction from project spaces for a specified period
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all project spaces

The severity of consequences will be determined based on:
- Intent of the behavior
- Impact on the community
- History of previous violations
- Willingness to correct the behavior

## Educational Context

This project exists to help students develop critical thinking skills. Contributors should:
- Remember that students and educators may read contributions
- Maintain professional and appropriate language at all times
- Consider the educational value of their contributions
- Prioritize accuracy and pedagogical soundness in claims and content
